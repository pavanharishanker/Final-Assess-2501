The Death Rate

0001-01-01

A Growing Number Of Deaths?

At first glance of the data, for the period of 1980-2018, there has been a rise in total deaths from all causes in the NSW Region. Where, over-time in the scatter plot below, there is a clear increasing trend in the total numbers of deaths relative to time.

And Yet A Declining Death Rate

Indeed, whilst interpretation of the first data visualization may communicate that the total death may be rising. In regards to tackling the concept of an Ageing population, we must focus upon the rate of death, rather than its total numeric value.

Supported By Evidence Of A Increasing Life Expectancy

Conclusion On The Contribution Of Death Rates To An Ageing Population

Indeed from analysis of the above scatter-plot, we are able to derive, that whilst the total number of deaths may have risen during the 1980-2018 period, this was likely due to a increase in the total population of NSW rather than representing a greater rate of death.

This is validated by the strong negative growth of the death rate [per hundered thousand] over the period, revealing a declining death rate in NSW as a likely key contributor to its Ageing Population Crisis.
