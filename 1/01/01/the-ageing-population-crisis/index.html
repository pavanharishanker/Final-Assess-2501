The Ageing Population Crisis

0001-01-01

The Ageing Population Crisis, is a significant issue which effects the whole of the Australian population as well as the world as a whole. Indeed, it is estimated that in the period between 2015-2020, the world population of individuals ages 60+ will double from 12 to 22%.

Why This is Significant

This movement along the demographic transition model, results in a greater proportion of elderly relative to the working age population. Presenting, a greater dependency ratio and thus potential future limitations on economic growth capacity and productivity as well as significant changes to government policy to ease this transition. Overall, the aggregate result of policy and productivity changes will be felt by all individuals, thus making it a matter of interest.

My Purpose

Thus, in order to gain an understanding of what is causing this phenomenon, this blog will explore data from the NSW Health Stats Database, to divulge the fundamental drivers of this demographic change, providing a greater comprehension to its audience and ideation of potential solutions.
