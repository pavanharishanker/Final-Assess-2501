The NSW Population

0001-01-01

The Growing Total Population

Examining the first line plot “Total Population Growth”, we can clearly note a substantial increase in the total population for NSW. Serving as the key driver of the growing total death values explored in The Death Rate.

Furthermore, the notion of an ageing population, is reiterated in the second lineplot, displaying significant growth of the 85+ age population proportion within NSW, with expected further rises from 2020 onwards. As such, to further site evidence of an ageing population we must explore the Comparison of Population by Age.

Population Age Structure Comparison [1971-2017]

The Flattening Of The Population Age Structure

By examining the differences in population percentage distribtuions in relation to age across the two periods [1971 and 2017], we can clearly see the gradual flattening of the distribution, as the 50+ age brackets increase in proportion, representing the gradual shift in the NSW population demographic, towards that of an ageing population.

Further Visualization Of Age Distribution Transition [1971-2017]

So What Is Causing These Changes?

We can clearly see through the shift in the population distribution by age, that the NSW State and the broader region of Australia as a whole, is progressing into the fourth stage of the demographic transition model. In order to ascertain the cause of this, we will explore the two driving factors of [The Death Rate] and [The Fertility Rate].
