The Fertility Rate

0001-01-01

A Gradually Declining Fertility Rate

Exploring the Health Stats NSW data for the Total Fertility Rate [TFR] over the period of 2001 to 2019. We can see from the below plot, that whilst there was indeed a increase in the TFR during the 2005-2012 period. There has been significant decline in the Total Fertiltiy Rate in NSW from 2015 onwards. With a TFR of only 1.59 in 2019, a substantial 0.37 points below the all time high in 2007.

Coupled With A Resulting Decline In Working Age Population Growth

Conclusive Remarks

Thus, from examination of the above, we can see that a declining fertility and death rate, is contributing to a negative shift in the population age structure for the NSW State, with respects to productive capacity. The long-term impacts of which, are too early to measure as we begin this transition. However, if this trend towards an ageing population structure continues, there is undoubtable socioeconomic changes to arise as a corollary.
